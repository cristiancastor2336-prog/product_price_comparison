Product Price Comparison System: A Platform for Customers to Compare Prices Across Stores in Lambunao

Chapter 1 - Introduction

1.1 Background of the Study

The Product Price Comparison System is designed to help customers in Lambunao efficiently compare product prices across multiple stores, saving time and effort in finding the most affordable options. Currently, many customers rely on traditional shopping methods, personally visiting several stores to check prices and availability. This approach is often inconvenient, time-consuming, and costly, making it difficult for buyers to make well-informed purchasing decisions.

At the same time, local sellers primarily depend on walk-in customers, word-ofmouth, and occasional social media posts to promote their products. Product information is scattered across different platforms and often not organized in a searchable or accessible manner. As a result, potential buyers may not easily discover available products or updated prices from various stores, while sellers face challenges in reaching a broader customer base and promoting their offerings effectively.

The absence of a centralized system for price comparison creates multiple problems: customers may pay higher prices due to limited access to accurate and up-todate information, and the lack of summarized insights, such as identifying the lowest price for a product, makes decision-making inefficient. Similarly, sellers have reduced visibility, limiting their market reach and competitiveness, especially for small local businesses that rely solely on physical store presence.

To address these challenges, the proposed Product Price Comparison System provides a digital platform that integrates the needs of both customers and sellers. Customers can easily search for products, compare prices across multiple stores, and view helpful insights, such as the lowest available price. Sellers can upload and manage their product listings, ensuring accurate information and improved visibility to potential buyers.

By streamlining the buying and selling process, this system promotes informed decision-making, increases market competitiveness, and enhances the efficiency of the local marketplace in Lambunao, benefiting both consumers and local businesses.

1.2 Problem Statement and Objectives of the Study

Problem Statement

This study aims:

1. To develop a system that enables customers in Lambunao to easily search for and compare product prices from multiple local stores.
2. To reduce the time and effort customers spend visiting different stores to find the best prices, thereby improving convenience and purchasing efficiency.
3. To provide local sellers with a centralized platform where they can upload, update, and manage their product listings effectively.
4. To increase the visibility and reach of local sellers by allowing them to showcase their products to a wider group of potential buyers within the municipality.
5. To enhance the overall efficiency of the local marketplace by improving access to accurate price information, supporting informed purchasing decisions, and promoting fair competition and digital engagement among local businesses.

Objective of the Study

General Objectives

To develop a system that enables customers to compare product prices across multiple stores in Lambunao, allowing them to make informed purchasing decisions efficiently.

Specific Objectives

1. To provide a platform where customers can easily search for products and view prices from different stores.
2. To allow sellers to upload and manage their product listings, including updating prices and product details.
3. To reduce the time and effort customers spend visiting multiple stores to find the best price.
4. To increase visibility and reach for local sellers by showcasing their products to potential buyers.
5. To generate insights or summaries for customers, such as highlighting the lowest price for a given product.

1.3 Scope, Delimitation, and Significance

Scope

The study will develop a web-based Product Price Comparison System for customers and local sellers in Lambunao. The system will allow customers to search for products and compare prices from different participating stores, helping them make faster and more informed purchasing decisions.

It will also provide features for sellers to upload, update, and manage their product listings, ensuring that product information and prices remain accurate and accessible. In addition, the system will generate useful insights, such as identifying the lowest available price for a specific product.

The platform will support three types of users: customers, sellers, and a system administrator. By improving access to organized price information and simplifying product comparison, the system aims to enhance convenience for buyers and increase visibility and efficiency for local sellers in the marketplace.

Delimitation

The study is limited to developing a Product Price Comparison System that focuses only on displaying product information, price listings, and comparison features for selected local stores in Lambunao. The system will help customers compare prices and allow sellers to manage their product listings within a centralized platform.

It will not include online payment processing, product delivery services, logistics tracking, or coverage of stores outside the municipality. The system is not intended to function as a full e-commerce platform but will concentrate mainly on improving price comparison and product visibility at the local level.

Significance of the Study

This study is important because it provides benefits to different groups of people and organizations involved in the local marketplace. For users or customers, the system offers a faster and more convenient way to compare prices from multiple stores in Lambunao. By allowing customers to view and evaluate product prices online, they can make better-informed purchasing decisions while saving time, effort, and transportation costs that would otherwise be spent visiting several stores.

For local sellers or stores, the system helps increase the visibility of their products to a wider audience. By managing product listings through a centralized platform, sellers can reach more potential buyers, promote their items more effectively, and compete fairly in the local market. This can lead to increased sales opportunities and better engagement with customers.

The study also benefits developers, as creating the system allows them to enhance their skills in areas such as web development, database management, and user interface design. It provides practical experience in solving real-world problems by applying technical knowledge to address the needs of both buyers and sellers.

Finally, the study is valuable to future researchers, as it can serve as a reference for further studies related to e-commerce systems, digital marketplaces, and product price comparison platforms. The insights and methods developed in this research can guide similar projects and contribute to the ongoing improvement of local and regional digital commerce solutions.

3.1 Development Framework

Project Development Model Used

The development of the Product Price Comparison System for Lambunao will use the Agile Model. Agile is chosen because it allows iterative and incremental development, making it easier to adapt to changes based on user feedback. Since the system involves multiple users, customers, and sellers with varying needs, Agile ensures continuous improvement and testing throughout the development process. This approach aligns with the objectives of creating a user-friendly platform for price comparison, product listing management, and generating helpful insights.

SDLC Phases Applied

Planning

The planning phase identifies the project's scope, goals, and requirements. This system, it involves understanding the current challenges in Lambunao: customers spending time visiting multiple stores and sellers struggling to reach buyers. Planning includes defining features such as product search, price comparison, seller listing management, and price summary insights.

Analysis

During analysis, the requirements of both customers and sellers are gathered in detail. This involves studying how buyers compare prices and what information sellers need to manage their listings. Potential problems, such as scattered product information and inefficient decision-making, are analyzed to ensure the system addresses these issues effectively.

The design phase focuses on creating the architecture of the system. It includes designing user interfaces for customers to search products and view prices, interfaces for sellers to upload and update listings, and administrative functions to manage the platform. Development

In Agile, development occurs in iterative cycles called sprints. Features such as product search, price comparison, seller listing management, and insight generation are built incrementally. Each sprint produces a working version of the system that can be reviewed, tested, and improved in the next iteration.

Testing

Testing is performed continuously during each sprint to identify and fix errors. Functional testing ensures that product search, price comparison, and listing management work correctly. Usability testing ensures that the system is intuitive for both customers and sellers, and feedback from actual users is used to make improvements

Deployment

After successful testing of each module, the system is deployed for real use in Lambunao. Customers can access the platform to search and compare products, while sellers can upload and manage their listings. Deployment is incremental, allowing updates and improvements without interrupting the system.

Maintenance

Maintenance in Agile is ongoing. The system is continuously monitored and updated based on user feedback and changing requirements. Improvements may include adding new features, optimizing performance, or addressing the emerging needs of customers and sellers to ensure the system remains relevant and effective.

3.3 System Design

System Architecture

This diagram illustrates a standard Three-Tier Web Architecture, a structural model used to build modern, scalable web applications by separating concerns into distinct layers: presentation, logic, and data.

This layer consists of the web interface the visual part of the application that users see in their browsers. It is built using foundational web technologies like HTML for structure, CSS and Bootstrap for styling and responsiveness, and JavaScript for interactivity. When a user performs an action, such as clicking a button or submitting a form, the Frontend sends a "Request" to the next layer.

The Backend (Application Layer) serves as the "brain" of the operation. Running on an application server, it uses PHP to process the logic of the request. This layer acts as a middleman; it receives the request from the frontend, determines what needs to happen (like verifying a password or calculating a total), and communicates with the database. It often utilizes REST APIs to ensure that data is transferred efficiently and securely between the different parts of the system.

Finally, the Database (Data Storage Layer) is where all permanent information is kept. Using a management system like MySQL, this layer organizes data into tables. When the Backend needs to save new information or retrieve existing records, it communicates with this storage layer. Once the data is processed, the Backend sends a "Response" back to the Frontend, which then updates the display for the user.

In summary, this architecture ensures a smooth flow of information: users trigger actions on the Frontend, which are processed by the Backend, which then stores or fetches information from the Database to complete the cycle. By separating these functions, developers can update the design of the website without breaking the underlying logic or risking the integrity of the stored data.

3.4 System Implementation

Frontend Development

The Frontend, or Presentation Layer, acts as the primary Web Interface where users interact with the system. Built using HTML for structural integrity and CSS for visual styling, it incorporates the Bootstrap framework to ensure a responsive design that functions seamlessly across various devices. JavaScript is utilized to implement dynamic features such as real-time search filters and instant price comparisons. A critical focus of this layer is inclusivity; by leveraging these standard web technologies and integrating accessibility features like keyboard navigation, the system ensures that all citizens, regardless of their technical abilities, can navigate the local marketplace effectively.

Backend Development

The Backend serves as the Application Server, acting as the "brain" of the operation. This layer is powered by PHP, which handles the server-side processing of all incoming user requests. To facilitate smooth communication between the visual interface and the underlying data, the system employs REST APIs. These APIs ensure that when a customer requests a price comparison, the Backend accurately interprets the query, performs the necessary business logic such as sorting prices from various stores and delivers a structured response back to the Frontend.

Database and Security Features

At the foundation of this architecture is the Database, or Storage Layer, which utilizes MySQL for permanent data management. This layer is responsible for the secure storage of product listings, store details, and user profiles. The Backend communicates directly with this layer to store data or retrieve records needed to fulfill a request. To protect this data, a multi-layered security approach is integrated into the architecture. Authentication identifies users through unique credentials, while role-based Authorization ensures that only Admins and Owners have the permissions to modify sensitive data, leaving Customers with read-only access for comparisons. Finally, rigorous Data Validation is performed by the Backend on all inputs received from the Frontend. This ensures that only clean, accurate information is committed to the MySQL database, maintaining the overall integrity and trustworthiness of the Lambunao Price Comparison System.
